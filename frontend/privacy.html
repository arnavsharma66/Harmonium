Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

Harmonium is a personal music player that connects to your YouTube account to play your playlists and liked songs. This policy explains what data we access and how we handle it.

What we access

When you sign in with Google, Harmonium requests read-only access to your YouTube account. Specifically:

  • Your YouTube playlists and their contents
  • Your liked videos
  • Your basic Google profile (name, email, profile picture) to display your account info

What we do not do

  • We do not store your YouTube data on any server
  • We do not share your data with any third party
  • We do not modify your YouTube account in any way
  • We do not collect analytics or track your usage
  • We do not sell or monetise any data

How your data is used

Your Google OAuth token is stored temporarily in server memory to make YouTube API calls on your behalf. It is never written to a database or persistent storage. When you sign out, the token is discarded immediately.

Play history, liked track IDs, and preferences are stored locally in your browser's localStorage and never leave your device.

YouTube API

Harmonium uses the YouTube Data API v3. Your use of Harmonium is therefore also subject to YouTube's Terms of Service and Google's Privacy Policy.

You can revoke Harmonium's access to your Google account at any time by visiting Google Account Permissions.

Third-party services

Harmonium fetches lyrics from LRCLIB, a free and open lyrics database. No identifying information is sent — only the song title and artist name.
